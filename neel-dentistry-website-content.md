### SERVICE 1 — General Dentistry

**Icon:** Tooth / Shield
**H4 Title:** General Dentistry
**Card Copy:**
Your foundation for lifelong oral health. From routine check-ups and professional cleanings to fillings and gum care — we keep your smile healthy every day, not just when something goes wrong.

**Service Page Headline:**
Everyday Care for a Lifetime of Healthy Smiles

**Service Page Body:**
General dentistry is the cornerstone of everything we do at Neel Dentistry. Regular visits aren't just about cleaning — they're how we catch small problems before they become expensive ones. Our general dentistry services are thorough, gentle, and built around your schedule.

**What We Offer — Included Treatments:**
- Comprehensive Oral Examinations
- Professional Teeth Cleaning (Scaling & Polishing)
- Tooth-Coloured Fillings
- Root Canal Treatment
- Tooth Extractions
- Gum Disease Diagnosis & Treatment (Periodontics)
- Digital X-Rays (low radiation)
- Oral Cancer Screening
- Night Guards & Mouthguards
- Bad Breath (Halitosis) Assessment & Treatment

**What to Expect — Step by Step:**
1. **Examination** — We review your dental history and take digital X-rays to get a complete picture of your oral health.
2. **Assessment** — We walk you through our findings honestly, with no jargon. You see exactly what we see.
3. **Treatment Planning** — We discuss your options, prioritise by urgency, and build a plan that fits your life and budget.
4. **Treatment** — Comfortable, efficient, and explained every step of the way.
5. **Recall & Prevention** — We set you up with a recall schedule so small issues never become big ones.

**Why It Matters:**
Oral health is directly linked to your overall wellbeing. Heart disease, diabetes, and pregnancy complications have all been associated with untreated dental conditions. A twice-yearly visit to Neel Dentistry is one of the simplest and most effective things you can do for your total health — not just your smile.

**FAQ — General Dentistry:**

*Q: How often should I visit the dentist?*
A: We recommend a check-up and cleaning every 6 months for most patients. Some patients with gum disease or a history of decay may benefit from visits every 3–4 months. We'll tell you what's right for you specifically.

*Q: Do cleanings hurt?*
A: A professional cleaning should not be painful. You may feel mild sensitivity, especially around the gumline. If you experience discomfort during your appointment, tell us immediately — we adjust.

*Q: I haven't been to a dentist in years. Will you judge me?*
A: Never. We see patients at every stage of dental health. Our job is to help you move forward, not to make you feel bad about the past. The best time to start was years ago — the second best time is now.

### SERVICE 5 — Emergency Dental Care

**Icon:** Cross / Urgency symbol
**H4 Title:** Emergency Dental Care
**Card Copy:**
Dental emergencies don't wait for convenient timing. Whether it's a broken tooth, severe pain, or a knocked-out tooth — we reserve same-day appointments for patients who need us most.

**Service Page Headline:**
When It Can't Wait — We're Here

**Service Page Body:**
A dental emergency is frightening, painful, and often happens at the worst possible moment. At Neel Dentistry, we keep same-day slots available every day for genuine dental emergencies. Call us. Describe what's happening. We will see you.

**What Counts as a Dental Emergency:**
- Severe, persistent toothache
- Knocked-out or dislodged tooth
- Cracked or fractured tooth causing pain
- Lost filling or crown
- Dental abscess or swelling
- Facial swelling linked to dental infection
- Bleeding that won't stop after an extraction
- Broken braces wire causing injury

**What to Do Before You Arrive:**

*For a knocked-out tooth:*
Handle only by the crown (white part). Do not scrub it. Place it in milk or hold it between your cheek and gum. Get to us within 30–60 minutes — this is critical for re-implantation success.

*For severe pain:*
Take your normal pain relief if applicable. Do not apply aspirin directly to the gum. Call us immediately.

*For swelling:*
Apply a cold pack to the outside of your cheek (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off). Do not apply heat. Swelling linked to dental infection requires urgent professional attention.

**What to Expect:**
1. **Call us first** — Describe your situation so we can prioritise your appointment and prepare appropriately.
2. **Same-day assessment** — We examine, diagnose, and stabilise your condition immediately.
3. **Immediate treatment or referral** — We treat what we can on the spot. If specialist care is needed, we arrange it for you directly.
4. **Follow-up plan** — We schedule your follow-up before you leave so your recovery is fully supported.

**FAQ — Emergency Care:**

*Q: Do I need to be an existing patient?*
A: No. We see emergency patients regardless of whether you've visited us before.

*Q: What are your emergency hours?*
A: We offer same-day emergency appointments during clinic hours. For after-hours emergencies, call our number — we have an on-call protocol for urgent cases.

### SERVICE 6 — Dental Implants

**Icon:** Implant / Anchor symbol
**H4 Title:** Dental Implants
**Card Copy:**
A missing tooth isn't just a cosmetic concern — it affects how you eat, speak, and how your remaining teeth align. Implants are the closest thing to a natural tooth that modern dentistry can offer.

**Service Page Headline:**
Permanent. Natural. Life-Changing.

**Service Page Body:**
Dental implants are the gold standard for replacing missing teeth. Unlike dentures or bridges, implants are anchored directly into the jawbone — providing stability, preventing bone loss, and looking and functioning exactly like natural teeth. If you've been missing a tooth for any length of time, this conversation is worth having.

**What We Offer:**
- Single Tooth Implants
- Multiple Teeth Implants
- Implant-Supported Dentures
- Bone Grafting (where required)
- Full Treatment Planning & 3D Imaging

**What to Expect:**
1. **Implant Consultation** — Full assessment including 3D imaging to evaluate bone density and placement position.
2. **Preparation (if needed)** — Bone grafting or tooth extraction if required before implant placement.
3. **Implant Placement** — The titanium post is placed into the jawbone under local anaesthesia. Healing takes 3–6 months.
4. **Abutment & Crown** — Once healed, the abutment and custom-made crown are attached. Final result looks and functions like your natural tooth.
5. **Long-Term Care** — Implants are cared for exactly like natural teeth — brushing, flossing, and regular check-ups.

**FAQ — Implants:**

*Q: Am I a candidate for implants?*
A: Most healthy adults are. Key factors are adequate bone density and good overall health. We assess this thoroughly at your consultation and will tell you honestly if implants are right for you.

*Q: How long do implants last?*
A: With proper care, implants can last a lifetime. The crown on top may need replacement after 15–20 years.

*Q: Is the procedure painful?*
A: The procedure is done under local anaesthesia — you won't feel pain during placement. Post-procedure discomfort is manageable with standard pain relief and typically subsides within a few days.

## PATIENT INFO — FAQs Section

**Eyebrow Label:**
Frequently Asked Questions

**H3 Heading:**
Questions We Hear Every Day —
Answered Honestly

**Intro Copy:**
We've compiled the questions patients ask us most. If yours isn't here, just call us — there's no such thing as a question too small.

---

**Tab 1 — General**

*Q: How do I know if I need to see a dentist urgently?*
A: See us urgently if you have: severe or persistent toothache, visible swelling in the face or gums, a tooth that has been knocked out, bleeding that won't stop, or difficulty opening your mouth or swallowing. If in doubt — call us. We'd rather you call and it not be an emergency than not call and it be one.

*Q: Can I change my dentist to Neel Dentistry?*
A: Absolutely. You can register as a new patient at any time. If you'd like us to contact your previous dental practice for your records, we can arrange that with your permission.

*Q: How do I know what treatment I actually need?*
A: We will always explain our clinical findings and the reason behind every recommendation. You are entitled to ask why, ask for alternatives, and take time to decide. We encourage that.

*Q: Is it safe to visit the dentist if I'm pregnant?*
A: Yes, and we recommend it. Pregnancy can affect gum health significantly. Routine check-ups and cleanings are safe throughout pregnancy. We adjust our approach for X-rays and certain medications — always tell us you're pregnant at the start of your appointment.

*Q: What if I haven't been to a dentist in a long time?*
A: You will not be judged. Many of our patients come to us after years away from dental care. We start with a thorough assessment, explain everything clearly, and build a plan that addresses your needs without overwhelming you.

---

**Tab 2 — Services**

*Q: What's the difference between a filling and a root canal?*
A: A filling treats decay that hasn't reached the nerve of the tooth. A root canal is needed when decay or infection has reached the inner pulp of the tooth. Root canals have an unfair reputation — with modern techniques and anaesthesia, they're no more uncomfortable than a standard filling.

*Q: How long does teeth whitening last?*
A: In-chair whitening typically lasts 12–18 months depending on your diet and habits (coffee, tea, red wine, and smoking all stain teeth faster). Take-home trays can be used for top-up treatments to maintain results.

*Q: Can I get veneers if my teeth aren't perfectly aligned?*
A: Sometimes. It depends on the degree of misalignment. In some cases, we recommend orthodontic treatment first to give you the best possible foundation for veneers. We'll assess this at your consultation and give you an honest recommendation.

*Q: How do I know if my child needs braces?*
A: We typically screen for orthodontic issues from age 7. Signs to look out for include crowded teeth, early or late loss of baby teeth, difficulty biting or chewing, and jaws that shift or make sounds. Bring your child in and we'll assess — early treatment often means simpler, shorter treatment later.

---

**Tab 3 — Payments**

*Q: Do you offer payment plans?*
A: Yes, for qualifying treatments. We offer structured payment schedules for larger procedures such as implants, orthodontics, and smile makeovers. Speak to our front desk team to understand what's available for your specific treatment plan.

*Q: Can I get a cost estimate before committing to treatment?*
A: Always. We provide a written treatment plan with full cost breakdown before any work begins. You will never be surprised by a bill at Neel Dentistry.

*Q: What if my insurance doesn't cover a treatment I need?*
A: We'll explain exactly what's covered and what isn't, then discuss your payment options for the uncovered portion. We believe financial limitations shouldn't mean compromising on necessary care — and we'll work with you to find a path forward.

*Q: Do you charge for a consultation?*
A: Please call our clinic for current consultation fee information. We are transparent about all fees upfront.

---

**Tab 4 — Kids**

*Q: My child is scared of the dentist. What should I do before the first visit?*
A: Talk about the dentist matter-of-factly — not as something to "be brave" for. Avoid phrases like "it won't hurt" as this can increase anxiety. Books and videos about dental visits can help. And when you arrive, let our team take it from there — we're experienced in working with anxious children at every level.

*Q: At what age should my child start using toothpaste?*
A: A small smear of fluoride toothpaste can be used as soon as the first tooth appears. From age 3, use a pea-sized amount. Always supervise brushing until age 7–8 when children have the motor skills to do it effectively on their own.

*Q: Are baby teeth important if they're going to fall out anyway?*
A: Critically important. Baby teeth hold space for adult teeth, affect speech development, and influence jaw growth. Decay in baby teeth can also spread and affect developing adult teeth underneath. We care for baby teeth with the same attention as adult teeth.

*Q: How can I prevent cavities in my child's teeth?*
A: Four things make the biggest difference: fluoride toothpaste twice daily, limiting sugary drinks and snacks, regular dental check-ups from an early age, and fissure sealants — a protective coating we apply to the biting surfaces of back teeth, which is one of the most effective cavity prevention tools available.
